Things To Do

Cantacuzino Castle itself is one of the things to do in the region. As you will see this on the day / night of the wedding you will be finding some more recommendations below.

Peleș Castle


2 Aleea Peleșului Sinaia, PH 106100 Romania +40 244 310 918

One of the most beautiful castles in the country. Intact and well kept, this place used to be the summer residence of the Royal family.

Sinaia Monastery



leea Peleșului Sinaia, 2 Strada Mănăstirii Sinaia, PH 106100 Romania +40 244 314 917

The monastery gave its name to the nearby town of Sinaia. The monastery consists of two courtyards surrounded by low buildings. In the centre of each courtyard there is a small church built in the Byzantine style. One of them—"Biserica Veche" (The Old Church)—dates from 1695, while the more recent "Biserica Mare" (The Great Church) was built in 1846.

Bucegi National Park


DB Romania +40 372 758 550

These impressive mountains will be the backdrop of our wedding. Bucegi National Park covers an area of 32,663 hectares across three counties, more than 60% of the natural park is covered with forests. Perfect for hiking, mountain biking, visiting waterfalls and discovering strange rock formations and you might even see some bears.

Sfinx


DJ714 DB Romania +40 727 475 985

On the Bucegi plateau you can find this rock formation shrouded in mystery and conspiracy theories. The odd formation is one of the “Seven Wonders of Romania". You can hike all the way up to check it out, or take the cable car from Bușteni.

Heroes' Cross on Caraiman Peak


PH Romania

The Heroes' Cross is a monument built in the 1920s at the height of the Oriental Express on Caraiman Peak at 2,291 m. You will be able to see it from Cantacuzino castle, but if you want to get up close you can take a cable car from Bușteni and then it is a 1 hour walk following the red cross markings on the relatively flat plateau.

Howling Waterfall


PH Romania

The Urlatoarea Waterfall can be found on an easy trail that is marked with red circles and starts from the cable car base station in Bușteni. The hike is about 2 hours (4.5 km) round trip.

"George Enescu" Classical Music Festival


Bucharest, various locations

The George Enescu Festival (August - September), held in honor of the celebrated Romanian composer George Enescu, is the biggest classical music festival and classical international competition held in Romania and one of the biggest in Eastern Europe.

Palace of the People


2-4 Strada Izvor București, Municipiul București Romania

Built during the communist regime, the formerly known House of the People (now the Palace of the Parliament) holds many famous and infamous records: the world's largest civilian building with an administrative function (the seat of the Parliament of Romania), world's most expensive administrative building, world's heaviest building (4,098,500,000 kilograms). A visit gives you insight into the megalomaniac mind of Ceaușescu. Interesting fact: the building has just as many levels underground as above.

Libearty Bear Sanctuary Zarnesti


Zărnești, BV Romania +40 268 471 202

You still find wild bears in Romania, but you do not want to come across them on a hike. If you want to see these magnificent animals in a safe place, but not in a zoo, this is the place to go.

Brașov


Brașov, BV Romania

The closest city, historical with nice architecture, surrounded by mountains. There are many things to do. We recommend the restaurant "La Musica" and the Black Church.

Bran Castle


24 Strada General Traian Moșoiu Bran, BV 507025 Romania +40 268 237 700

Dracula's legend? Learn more about how the myth came about. It's located in Bran, a touristy town next to Piatra Craiului National Park (also a great place for hiking). By car it's 45 minutes from Busteni. By public transport it's a bit difficult but not impossible: go by train to Brasov and from there take a bus.

Râșnov Citadel


Râșnov Romacril, BV 505400 Romania

Rasnov Citadel is well worth a visit and one of the few citadels of its kind. The fortress was its own small community and served as a place of refuge and safety during times of attack. All classes of people, including peasants from nearby villages, fled to the fortress and stayed in the small houses lining the spiraling streets inside.
