Things To Do


Bușteni and Sinaia | Brașov and surroundings | Sibiu and Cluj | Bucharest

Cantacuzino Castle itself is one of the things to visit in the region. The impressive mountains forming the backdrop of our wedding are part of the Bucegi National Park. Covering an area of 32,663 hectares across three counties, more than 60% of the natural park is covered with forests. Perfect for hiking, mountain biking, visiting waterfalls and discovering strange rock formations.

Bușteni and Sinaia

Peleș Castle
One of the most beautiful castles in the country. Intact and well kept, this place used to be the summer residence of the Royal family.
Sinaia Monastery
The monastery gave its name to the nearby town of Sinaia. The monastery consists of two courtyards surrounded by low buildings. In the centre of each courtyard there is a small church built in the Byzantine style. One of them—"Biserica Veche" (The Old Church)—dates from 1695, while the more recent "Biserica Mare" (The Great Church) was built in 1846.
The Sphinx
On the Bucegi plateau you can find this natural rock formation shrouded in mystery and conspiracy theories. The odd formation is one of the “Seven Wonders of Romania". You can hike all the way up to check it out, or take the cable car from Bușteni.
Heroes' Cross on Caraiman Peak
The Heroes' Cross is a monument built in the 1920s at the height of the Oriental Express on Caraiman Peak at 2,291 m. You will be able to see it from Cantacuzino castle, but if you want to get up close you can take a cable car from Bușteni and then it is a 1 hour walk following the red cross markings on the relatively flat plateau.

Brașov and surroundings

Brașov
The closest city, historical with nice architecture, surrounded by mountains. There are many things to do. We recommend the restaurant "La Musica" and the Black Church.
Bran Castle
Dracula's legend? Learn more about how the myth came about. It's located in Bran, a touristy town next to Piatra Craiului National Park (also a great place for hiking). By car it's 45 minutes from Busteni. By public transport it's a bit difficult but not impossible: go by train to Brasov and from there take a bus.
Râșnov Citadel
Râșnov Citadel is well worth a visit and one of the few citadels of its kind. The fortress was its own small community and served as a place of refuge and safety during times of attack. All classes of people, including peasants from nearby villages, fled to the fortress and stayed in the small houses lining the spiraling streets inside.
Libearty Bear Sanctuary Zărnești
You still find wild bears in Romania, but you do not want to come across them on a hike. If you want to see these magnificent animals in a safe place, but not in a zoo, this is the place to go.

Sibiu and Cluj

Sibiu
Sibiu (Hermannstadt in German) was the largest and wealthiest of the seven walled citadels* built in the 12th century by German settlers known as Transylvanian Saxons. Sibiu's Old Town retains the grandeur of its earlier days when rich and powerful guilds dominated regional trade. Sections of the medieval wall still guard the historic area, where narrow streets pass steep-roofed 17th century buildings with gable overhangs before opening into vast, church-dominated squares such as Great Square and Little Square. (source: romaniatourism.com)
Cluj-Napoca
This western city in the region of Transylvania traces its origins back to the Dacian settlement of Napuca in the 2-nd century A.D. After the Roman take-over of Dacia, it was renamed Napoca and in 124 A.D., received the rank of "municipium". German merchants, who arrived here in the 12th century, rebuilt the medieval earthen walls of Clus in stone after the Tartar invasion of 1241. With one of the most vibrant economies in the country and a population of around 330,000, Cluj, is today a vibrant cultural and educational city. (source: romaniatourism.com)

Bucharest

Palace of Parliament
Built during the communist regime, the formerly known House of the People (now the Palace of the Parliament) holds many famous and infamous records: the world's largest civilian building with an administrative function (the seat of the Parliament of Romania), world's most expensive administrative building, world's heaviest building (4,098,500,000 kilograms). A visit gives you insight into the megalomaniac mind of Ceaușescu. Interesting fact: the building has just as many levels underground as above. (image source: tripadvisor.com)
"George Enescu" Classical Music Festival
The George Enescu Festival (August - September), held in honor of the celebrated Romanian composer George Enescu, is the biggest classical music festival and classical international competition held in Romania and one of the biggest in Eastern Europe.
